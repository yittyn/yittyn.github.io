# July 19

## From AI Scientist to Indie Founder: Starting from Scratch

A quick intro: I've been in the AI field since 2017—almost a decade! Back during my bachelor's degree, when there was no hype and I had no idea what my future held, I did my final year project on computer vision for semiconductors. Since then, I've spent 6 years working as an AI scientist, focusing on training deterministic computer vision models (like CNNs).

When ChatGPT first blew up, I was skeptical. I doubted the "smartness" of the models, thinking they were just fancy calculators—glorified next-token prediction sandboxes that couldn't survive in the real world. So, I stayed away. 

But as the space matured and began delivering clear business value at a rapid pace, a wave of anxiety hit: I was lagging behind. 

So I finally dove in—and had my own "wow" moment. My friends teased me for being a late adopter, but hey, better late than never. To catch up with the trend, I decided to pursue a Master's degree (which I am currently enrolled in), focusing on frontier world models.

---

### The Pivot: Why a Product Builder?

With the recent rise of "one-person companies" (exciting) and tech layoffs (depressing), the idea of running my own business started creeping in. Back in college, I organized hackathons and built communities, but I never built actual products. Isn't it cool to be a founder?

This is my shot to challenge my limits, skip the 9-to-5 loop, and level up. As the saying goes, entrepreneurship forces you to become a better version of yourself.

This blog is where I'll document the journey—the experiments, the ups and downs, the mistakes, and the reflections.

---

### Starting from Scratch (on the Engineering Side)

Here's the catch: I rarely touch production-grade engineering. My career has been about conceptual R&D and quick Python proof-of-concepts. Building small deterministic models is completely different from building modern LLM applications, which is mostly software engineering (APIs, RAG, agents).

So, I'm basically starting engineering from scratch. I think it's worth sharing this journey publicly to help other aspiring builders. I want to highlight the messy parts, the failures, and the stupid mistakes. If you are struggling, I want you to know you're not alone.

Moving forward, I expect to cover:
* **AI Engineering:** LLMs, agents, RAG, etc.
* **Software Engineering:** Web, mobile, and systems.
* **Business:** Marketing, sales, and operations.

I won't just dump theoretical knowledge—there is already too much of that on the internet. Instead, I'll log **actions executed, tools explored, projects built, and my honest reflections** (with resources attached). I hope it sparks your curiosity or inspires you to build something too.

If you're looking for pure "vibe-coding" where AI does 100% of the work, this might not be the place for you. I prefer to work step-by-step and understand the foundations (at least the basics) so I feel confident and in control of what I build. 

That said, speed is critical. I'll force myself to ride the wave and leverage AI tools to speed up every part of the process. The goal is to build fast, launch fast, and fail fast.

> "If you want to succeed, double your failure rate." — Thomas J. Watson

Thanks for following along and keeping me accountable. Hopefully, I'll be sharing some product announcements here soon!
